Building a keyboard

Step 1: Decide What Keyboard You Want

- Finding the Right Size Keyboard
- Picking Out The Features


Step 2: Pick Out the Parts

PCB, case, plate, stabilizers, switches, keycaps


Step 3: Get the Equipment Required

Switch puller, screwdriver kit, keycap puller


Step 4: Lube Your Switches (Optional)

You need to pull apart every switch and apply lube to the lower housing, spring, stem, and upper housing. The entire process takes a few hours, but it is a massive improvement in how the switches feel afterwards.


Step 6: Mod Your Stabilizers (Optional)

You can do a few quick changes to your stabilizers to greatly improve the sound and feel of them. Which includes clipping, lubing, and band-aid modding the stabilizers.


Step 7: Install Everything Into Plate & PCB

You need to press the switches into the plate and allow the pins to enter the PCB. Besides that, adding foam is an optional step, but it really improves the acoustics of your keyboard and adds some weight to the build.


Step 8: Install Assembled PCB Into Case

Install the PCB with the switches and stabilizers on it into the case


Step 9: Install the Keycaps

The keycaps are where everything starts to come together. Its quite the exciting process.
